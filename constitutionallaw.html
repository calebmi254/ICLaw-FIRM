Practice: Constitutional Law

Upholding the Core Principles of the Nation Through Law, Tradition, and Integrity

At ICL, we view Constitutional Law as more than a technical field—it embodies the fundamental agreement between the people and the state. It reflects the sovereignty of the nation, structures governance, and guides national unity. We approach the Constitution with respect and reverence, recognizing it as a document of heritage, restraint, and ongoing stewardship.

Constitutional Law comprises the set of rules, doctrines, and principles that determine the organization, powers, and limitations of government. It establishes:
  • The branches of government and their relationships
  • The rights and freedoms of individuals
  • The obligations of citizenship and boundaries of authority
  • The mechanisms for accountability, such as judicial review and constitutional interpretation
As the supreme law of the land, it overrides statutes, policies, and political considerations, serving as a guiding framework that promotes humility among leaders and dignity among citizens.

Our work is guided by the Transitional Constitution of South Sudan, which affirms:
  • Article 11 – The right to life and human dignity
  • Article 14 – Equality under the law
  • Article 19 – The right to a fair trial and due legal process
  • Article 26 – The duty to preserve unity and uphold constitutional order
  • Article 36 – The protection of culture, heritage, and identity
We interpret these provisions not only as legal requirements but also as mandates to foster the emotional and institutional well-being of the nation

Our practice aligns with key regional and international frameworks, including:
  • The African Charter on Human and Peoples’ Rights
  • The Revitalized Agreement on the Resolution of the Conflict in South Sudan (R-ARCSS)
  • The Universal Declaration of Human Rights
  • The Rome Statute of the International Criminal Court
  • The Geneva Conventions concerning the protection of civilians and combatants
We support efforts toward constitutional development, transitional justice, and the establishment of hybrid courts to address violations of both national and international law.

  • Legal Drafting and Advisory: Preparation of charters, declarations, and constitutional guidance
  • Judicial Review and Legal Interpretation: Assessing laws and government actions for constitutionality
  • Restorative Justice Initiatives: Facilitating mediation and issuing judgments rooted in constitutional principles
  • Civic Education and Youth Engagement: Promoting understanding of the Constitution as a foundation for unity and future stability
  • Institutional Reform and Governance Counseling: Assisting leaders with constitutional transitions and adherence to legal standards

We believe:
  • The Constitution is a living document that reflects the nation’s values and commitments
  • Law should be applied with emotional intelligence, spiritual awareness, and a focus on future generations
  • Justice entails restraint in power, clarity in counsel, and integrity in advocacy
CSR Initiative

Know Your Constitution Powered by ICL

To empower South Sudanese youth and communities with comprehensive knowledge of their constitutional rights and responsibilities, emphasizing the vital role of law in fostering national unity, dignity, and intergenerational reconciliation.

The Constitution as a Covenant
A foundational agreement between the people and the state; a framework for restraint, justice, and emotionally intelligent leadership.
Rights and Freedoms
Protecting life, dignity, equality, and fundamental freedoms; access to education, healthcare, and justice for all.
Duties of Citizenship
Promote unity, respect the rights of others, and support national heritage and public institutions with integrity.
A. Youth Constitution Circles
Regular meetings in schools and community centers facilitated by legal educators and youth representatives. Topics: “Understanding the Constitution,” “Justice and Restraint,” “Law as a Legacy.”
B. Pledge of Constitutional Guardianship
Formal Commitment
“In commitment to unity, dignity, and remembrance, I pledge to uphold the Constitution of South Sudan—not as a tool of division, but as a foundation of healing. I commit to protecting the rights of all citizens, acting with restraint, and leading my community with honor.”
C. Mobile Constitutional Outreach
Legal education in rural and flood-affected regions, simplified constitutional materials in local languages, and on-site civic ceremonies and mediation.
