Practice: Civil Liberties Law

Protecting the Breath of Freedom Through Restraint and Constitutional Grace

At ICL, Civil Liberties Law is not merely a shield against tyranny—it is the breath of personal dignity, the architecture of autonomy, and the ceremonial promise that no citizen shall be silenced, surveilled, or stripped of their humanity. We treat civil liberties as sacred rights that must be protected with humility, interpreted with emotional intelligence, and upheld with constitutional fidelity.

Civil liberties are the fundamental freedoms that protect individuals from government overreach. They are the rights that allow citizens to:
  • Speak freely without fear
  • Worship according to conscience
  • Assemble peacefully for justice
  • Maintain privacy and personal autonomy
  • Seek redress and due process under the law
These liberties are enshrined in South Sudan’s Transitional Constitution and aligned with international human rights law. They are not privileges—they are covenants.

Transitional Constitution of South Sudan:
  • Article 24 – Freedom of expression
  • Article 25 – Freedom of assembly and association
  • Article 27 – Freedom of religion
  • Article 19 – Right to fair trial and due process
  • Article 11 – Right to life and dignity

International instruments aligning with civil liberties:
  • Universal Declaration of Human Rights
  • African Charter on Human and Peoples’ Rights
  • International Covenant on Civil and Political Rights (ICCPR)
These instruments form the ceremonial and legal backbone of liberty in South Sudan and beyond.

  • Constitutional Advocacy and Litigation — Defending citizens against unlawful detention, censorship, and surveillance
  • Ceremonial Legal Drafting — Charters and declarations that elevate liberty into a language of remembrance
  • Youth Empowerment and Civic Education — Teaching civil liberties as tools of emotional resilience and generational protection
  • Restorative Justice and Public Mediation — Resolving liberty-related disputes with dignity and restraint
  • Legal Protection for Vulnerable Groups — Including journalists, activists, displaced persons, and religious minorities

We believe:
  • Liberty is not loud—it is sacred
  • Freedom is not chaos—it is covenant
  • Rights are not weapons—they are reminders of restraint
  • Justice must protect the soul, not just the body
CSR Initiative

Know Your Constitution Powered by ICL

To empower South Sudanese youth and communities with comprehensive knowledge of their constitutional rights and responsibilities, emphasizing the vital role of law in fostering national unity, dignity, and intergenerational reconciliation.

The Constitution as a Covenant
A foundational agreement between the people and the state; a framework for restraint, justice, and emotionally intelligent leadership.
Rights and Freedoms
Protecting life, dignity, equality, and fundamental freedoms; access to education, healthcare, and justice for all.
Duties of Citizenship
Promote unity, respect the rights of others, and support national heritage and public institutions with integrity.
A. Youth Constitution Circles
Regular meetings in schools and community centers facilitated by legal educators and youth representatives. Topics: “Understanding the Constitution,” “Justice and Restraint,” “Law as a Legacy.”
B. Pledge of Constitutional Guardianship
Formal Commitment
“In commitment to unity, dignity, and remembrance, I pledge to uphold the Constitution of South Sudan—not as a tool of division, but as a foundation of healing. I commit to protecting the rights of all citizens, acting with restraint, and leading my community with honor.”
C. Mobile Constitutional Outreach
Legal education in rural and flood-affected regions, simplified constitutional materials in local languages, and on-site civic ceremonies and mediation.
