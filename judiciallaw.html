Practice: Judicial Law

Upholding Justice through Integrity, Restraint, and Dignity

At ICL, Judiciary Law is more than the functioning of courts; it embodies the core principles of constitutional governance. It serves as a safeguard of independence, an interpreter of the law, and a foundation for justice and dignity in society. We view the judiciary not simply as an arbiter of disputes but as a vital institution dedicated to upholding constitutional values, fostering reconciliation, and ensuring the rule of law for future generations.

Judiciary Law establishes the framework, authority, and responsibilities of courts and judicial officers. Its key objectives include:
  • Maintaining judicial independence from political influence
  • Ensuring equitable access to justice for all citizens
  • Providing fair, impartial, and transparent dispute resolution
  • Interpreting and enforcing constitutional and statutory law
  • Overseeing administrative and executive actions through judicial review
In South Sudan, the judiciary plays a fundamental role within the Transitional Constitution, serving as a vital guardian of the rule of law and constitutional order.

Articles 123–138 of the Transitional Constitution of South Sudan:
  • Recognize the judiciary as an autonomous and independent entity
  • Define the judiciary’s structure: Supreme Court, Courts of Appeal, High Courts, County Courts
  • Ensure judicial independence, security of tenure, and adherence to high ethical standards
  • Guarantee citizens’ access to justice and the protection of fundamental rights

South Sudan operates a dual legal system:
  • Statutory Courts: Apply formal, codified law, predominantly in urban settings
  • Customary Courts: Operate under the Local Government Act, resolving disputes based on traditional norms and community dialogue
The judiciary bears the responsibility of harmonizing both systems and ensuring that customary judgments uphold constitutional principles and human rights standards.

  • Constitutional Litigation and Judicial Review — Challenging unlawful statutes, executive decisions, and administrative acts
  • Legal Drafting for Judicial and Ceremonial Purposes — Codes of conduct, judicial oaths, and frameworks for judicial integrity and reconciliation
  • Judicial Training and Capacity Building — Emotional intelligence, procedural fairness, and constitutional adherence for judges and magistrates
  • Judicial System Reform and Integration — Hybrid models combining statutory and customary justice mechanisms
  • Public Education and Access to Justice — Ensuring citizens can access judicial services with clarity and confidence

We are committed to the belief that:
  • Justice is rooted in fairness and dignity, not retribution
  • Courts serve as sanctuaries of impartiality and reflection
  • Judges function as custodians of restraint and constitutional fidelity
  • Judicial decisions should foster reconciliation, healing, and societal cohesion
CSR Initiative

Know Your Constitution Powered by ICL

To empower South Sudanese youth and communities with comprehensive knowledge of their constitutional rights and responsibilities, emphasizing the vital role of law in fostering national unity, dignity, and intergenerational reconciliation.

The Constitution as a Covenant
A foundational agreement between the people and the state; a framework for restraint, justice, and emotionally intelligent leadership.
Rights and Freedoms
Protecting life, dignity, equality, and fundamental freedoms; access to education, healthcare, and justice for all.
Duties of Citizenship
Promote unity, respect the rights of others, and support national heritage and public institutions with integrity.
A. Youth Constitution Circles
Regular meetings in schools and community centers facilitated by legal educators and youth representatives. Topics: “Understanding the Constitution,” “Justice and Restraint,” “Law as a Legacy.”
B. Pledge of Constitutional Guardianship
Formal Commitment
“In commitment to unity, dignity, and remembrance, I pledge to uphold the Constitution of South Sudan—not as a tool of division, but as a foundation of healing. I commit to protecting the rights of all citizens, acting with restraint, and leading my community with honor.”
C. Mobile Constitutional Outreach
Legal education in rural and flood-affected regions, simplified constitutional materials in local languages, and on-site civic ceremonies and mediation.
