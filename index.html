We Are ICL

In the Spirit of Justice, Heritage, and Commitment to Service and Law

Welcome to the Covenant House of Law and Justice, a dedicated institution committed to upholding ceremonial law, constitutional principles, and intergenerational stewardship. Inspired by the legacy of sovereignty and ancestral wisdom, our organization aims to serve as a conduit for national reconciliation and legal clarity in South Sudan.

We are more than legal practitioners; we are custodians of justice, advocates for moderation, and promoters of unity. Our work extends beyond litigation, embodying a sacred agreement between citizens and authorities, grounded in dignity, remembrance, and the integrity of our institutions.

To elevate the law from procedural necessity to a language that fosters unity, healing, and long-term protection. We provide clear counsel, advocate with integrity, and serve with humility. Our goal is to rebuild trust in public institutions, safeguard vulnerable communities, and guide leaders through transitional processes with constitutional integrity.

We operate within the following legal contexts:
  • The Transitional Constitution of South Sudan, emphasizing life, dignity, equality, and national unity
  • The African Charter on Human and Peoples’ Rights, promoting justice and freedom across the continent
  • International Humanitarian and Human Rights Law, including the Geneva Conventions and Rome Statute
  • Ceremonial Law, rooted in ancestral tradition and emotional intelligence, informing our approach to mediation, judgment, and public counsel
Our work aligns with the Hybrid Court for South Sudan and other transitional justice mechanisms that seek truth while emphasizing restraint.

  • Law as Heritage: Upholding the wisdom of ancestors and the aspirations of future generations
  • Justice as Moderation: Balancing ambition with humility and authority with compassion
  • Service as a Sacred Duty: An ongoing covenant rooted in trust and mutual respect
  • Unity as a Legacy: Transforming division into reconciliation and fostering constitutional maturity through dialogue and understanding

Our expertise includes:
  • Ceremonial legal drafting and constitutional advisory services
  • Restorative justice initiatives and public mediation
  • Humanitarian project development and pro bono legal support
  • Strategic counsel for national leadership and institutional reforms
  • Youth empowerment and inclusive governance initiatives

Based in Juba, South Sudan, our organization acts as a ceremonial bridge linking law with heritage, leadership with legacy. We welcome those who view justice as a means of healing and reconciliation, rather than conflict.

CSR Initiative

Know Your Constitution Powered by ICL

To empower South Sudanese youth and communities with comprehensive knowledge of their constitutional rights and responsibilities, emphasizing the vital role of law in fostering national unity, dignity, and intergenerational reconciliation.

The Constitution as a Covenant
A foundational agreement between the people and the state; a framework for restraint, justice, and emotionally intelligent leadership.
Rights and Freedoms
Protecting life, dignity, equality, and fundamental freedoms; access to education, healthcare, and justice for all.
Duties of Citizenship
Promote unity, respect the rights of others, and support national heritage and public institutions with integrity.
A. Youth Constitution Circles
Regular meetings in schools and community centers facilitated by legal educators and youth representatives. Topics: “Understanding the Constitution,” “Justice and Restraint,” “Law as a Legacy.”
B. Pledge of Constitutional Guardianship
Formal Commitment
“In commitment to unity, dignity, and remembrance, I pledge to uphold the Constitution of South Sudan—not as a tool of division, but as a foundation of healing. I commit to protecting the rights of all citizens, acting with restraint, and leading my community with honor.”
C. Mobile Constitutional Outreach
Legal education in rural and flood-affected regions, simplified constitutional materials in local languages, and on-site civic ceremonies and mediation.
