Practice: Customary Law

Integrating Traditional Wisdom with Modern Governance

At ICL, we view Customary Law not merely as historical tradition but as an active and vital framework that upholds remembrance, respect, and social cohesion. Grounded in community values and ancestral practices, it guides relationships, resolves disputes, and maintains dignity through oral traditions, rituals, and collective memory. We approach Customary Law as a complementary framework to statutory law, particularly in matters relating to land, marriage, inheritance, and community harmony.

Customary Law encompasses the unwritten rules, practices, and values passed down through successive generations to regulate community life. It is characterized by:
  • Oral and ancestral transmission: Communicated by elders, chiefs, and spiritual leaders
  • Restorative in nature: Focused on healing and reconciliation rather than punishment
  • Context-specific and community-centered: Adapted to local customs and cultural norms
  • Ceremonial and symbolic: Often enacted through rituals, blessings, and lineage-based authority
In South Sudan, Customary Law regulates over 80% of daily legal interactions, especially in rural and post-conflict environments.

South Sudan's legal system recognizes both statutory and customary laws, provided that customary practices are not incompatible with principles of justice, equity, and good conscience. Courts are permitted to refer to Customary Law in civil and land disputes, especially when statutory provisions are silent or inaccessible. The Transitional Constitution explicitly recognizes the role of Customary Law through:
  • Article 5: Affirming legal pluralism and traditional authority
  • Article 166: Recognizing the functions of traditional courts in local justice
  • Article 36: Protecting cultural heritage and lineage-based practices

Key areas where Customary Law is most actively applied include:
  • Land and communal resource management
  • Marriage, family relations, and inheritance
  • Community harmony and reconciliation processes
We ensure these domains are harmonized with statutory protections and constitutional safeguards.

  • Mediation and Reconciliation Facilitation — Led by community elders, spiritual leaders, and legal advisors
  • Land Dispute Resolution — Safeguarding customary land rights for returnees and vulnerable populations
  • Marriage and Inheritance Guidance — Harmonizing customary practices with statutory legal protections
  • Cultural Education and Youth Engagement — Promoting customary values as tools for social cohesion and emotional intelligence
  • Ensuring Compatibility with Statutory Law — Aligning customary rulings with constitutional principles and human rights standards

We hold that:
  • Customary practices represent a sacred covenant between generations
  • Justice involves remembrance and healing through ancestral wisdom
  • Law is rooted in lineage, influenced by elders’ guidance and the aspirations of future generations
  • Social unity is reinforced through ritual and reconciliation efforts
CSR Initiative

Know Your Constitution Powered by ICL

To empower South Sudanese youth and communities with comprehensive knowledge of their constitutional rights and responsibilities, emphasizing the vital role of law in fostering national unity, dignity, and intergenerational reconciliation.

The Constitution as a Covenant
A foundational agreement between the people and the state; a framework for restraint, justice, and emotionally intelligent leadership.
Rights and Freedoms
Protecting life, dignity, equality, and fundamental freedoms; access to education, healthcare, and justice for all.
Duties of Citizenship
Promote unity, respect the rights of others, and support national heritage and public institutions with integrity.
A. Youth Constitution Circles
Regular meetings in schools and community centers facilitated by legal educators and youth representatives. Topics: “Understanding the Constitution,” “Justice and Restraint,” “Law as a Legacy.”
B. Pledge of Constitutional Guardianship
Formal Commitment
“In commitment to unity, dignity, and remembrance, I pledge to uphold the Constitution of South Sudan—not as a tool of division, but as a foundation of healing. I commit to protecting the rights of all citizens, acting with restraint, and leading my community with honor.”
C. Mobile Constitutional Outreach
Legal education in rural and flood-affected regions, simplified constitutional materials in local languages, and on-site civic ceremonies and mediation.
