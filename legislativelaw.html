Practice: Legislative Law

Where Lawmaking Shapes the Future and Statutes Uphold Our Collective Values

At ICL, Legislative Law extends beyond mere bill drafting; it embodies the solemn responsibility of shaping a nation’s principles. It involves building a framework of restraint, fostering unity, and establishing laws that serve to protect and guide, rather than provoke or divide. We approach legislative drafting with the utmost respect, understanding that each clause bears significance, and every statute reflects our shared aspirations for the future.

Legislature Law pertains to the organization, powers, authority, and procedures of the national and state legislative bodies. Its primary objectives include:
  • Developing laws that align with constitutional principles and reflect public interests
  • Facilitating oversight of executive actions through inquiry and accountability measures
  • Ensuring effective representation of citizens through elected officials
  • Harmonizing statutory laws with customary and traditional norms
  • Safeguarding rights and upholding the integrity of the legislative process
In South Sudan, the National Legislature comprises the National Legislative Assembly and the Council of States, as established under Articles 55–70 of the Transitional Constitution.

Transitional Constitution of South Sudan:
  • Articles 55–70: Structure and authority of the National Legislature
  • Articles 71–76: Processes for lawmaking, presidential approval, and publication
  • Articles 77–78: Oversight functions and accountability mechanisms

  • Legislation enacted by the National Legislative Assembly
  • State laws harmonized with national statutes
  • Traditional declarations and ceremonial charters (where applicable)

  • Legislative Drafting and Formulation — Crafting statutes, charters, and declarations that promote coherence and unity
  • Constitutional Review and Alignment — Ensuring proposed legislation conforms with constitutional principles and societal values
  • Legislative Advisory and Oversight Support — Assisting lawmakers in maintaining transparency, restraint, and strategic governance
  • Civic Engagement and Educational Programs — Promoting understanding of the legislative process to foster inclusion and national cohesion
  • Integration of Traditional and Customary Laws — Designing hybrid frameworks that incorporate indigenous wisdom within statutory systems

We believe:
  • Law is a process of solemnity and purpose, not excess
  • Legislation is a covenant with the people, rooted in shared values
  • Parliament serves as a sanctuary for remembrance and national identity
  • Every law should serve to protect the foundational spirit of the nation, beyond mere structure
CSR Initiative

Know Your Constitution Powered by ICL

To empower South Sudanese youth and communities with comprehensive knowledge of their constitutional rights and responsibilities, emphasizing the vital role of law in fostering national unity, dignity, and intergenerational reconciliation.

The Constitution as a Covenant
A foundational agreement between the people and the state; a framework for restraint, justice, and emotionally intelligent leadership.
Rights and Freedoms
Protecting life, dignity, equality, and fundamental freedoms; access to education, healthcare, and justice for all.
Duties of Citizenship
Promote unity, respect the rights of others, and support national heritage and public institutions with integrity.
A. Youth Constitution Circles
Regular meetings in schools and community centers facilitated by legal educators and youth representatives. Topics: “Understanding the Constitution,” “Justice and Restraint,” “Law as a Legacy.”
B. Pledge of Constitutional Guardianship
Formal Commitment
“In commitment to unity, dignity, and remembrance, I pledge to uphold the Constitution of South Sudan—not as a tool of division, but as a foundation of healing. I commit to protecting the rights of all citizens, acting with restraint, and leading my community with honor.”
C. Mobile Constitutional Outreach
Legal education in rural and flood-affected regions, simplified constitutional materials in local languages, and on-site civic ceremonies and mediation.
