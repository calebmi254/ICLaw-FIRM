Executive Law

Where Authority Is Exercised with Accountability and Leadership Serves as a Foundation

Practice: Executive Law

Where Authority Is Exercised with Accountability and Leadership Serves as a Foundation

At ICL, our approach to Executive Law emphasizes responsible governance and service. It delineates the conduct, duties, and limitations of national leaders, ensuring decisions uphold legal integrity and constitutional fidelity. We view executive authority as a commitment to restraint, constitutional principles, and national stability, with every action aimed at safeguarding the nation's well-being.

Executive Law clarifies the powers, responsibilities, and ethical standards for the President, Vice Presidents, Ministers, and other senior officials. Its primary objectives include:
  • Ensuring lawful implementation of national policies
  • Protecting constitutional rights and societal dignity
  • Maintaining accountability to the legislature and judiciary
  • Providing stable leadership during transitions and crises
  • Exercising restraint in the use of force, public resources, and communication
In South Sudan, the executive authority is established in Articles 97–122 of the Transitional Constitution and operates within a framework of decentralization, ceremonial responsibilities, and constitutional oversight.

Transitional Constitution of South Sudan:
  • Articles 97–122 – Structure and powers of the Executive
  • Article 101 – Responsibilities of the President
  • Articles 112–114 – Appointment and conduct of Ministers
  • Articles 118–120 – Code of conduct, conflict of interest, and oath of office

  • Public Financial Management and Accountability Act
  • Civil Service Act
  • Anti-Corruption Commission Act
  • National Security Service Act
  • Protocols for ceremonial procedures and executive restraint

  • Legal Drafting for Ceremonial Official Functions — Preparation of oaths, declarations, and policy documents promoting unity
  • Constitutional Advisory and Oversight Assistance — Ensuring executive actions adhere to legal standards, dignity, and emotional intelligence
  • Leadership Transition Protocols — Developing ceremonial procedures and frameworks to facilitate smooth transitions and reconciliation
  • Youth Engagement and Civic Education — Promoting understanding of executive responsibilities as a form of community stewardship
  • Public Communication and Institutional Brand Management — Supporting transparent and unified messaging that reinforces national unity and stability

We believe:
  • Leadership is a commitment to service and integrity
  • Authority comes with responsibility and restraint
  • The executive is bound by constitutional law and not above it
  • Every decision should aim to uphold the core values and dignity of the nation
CSR Initiative

Know Your Constitution Powered by ICL

To empower South Sudanese youth and communities with comprehensive knowledge of their constitutional rights and responsibilities, emphasizing the vital role of law in fostering national unity, dignity, and intergenerational reconciliation.

The Constitution as a Covenant
A foundational agreement between the people and the state; a framework for restraint, justice, and emotionally intelligent leadership.
Rights and Freedoms
Protecting life, dignity, equality, and fundamental freedoms; access to education, healthcare, and justice for all.
Duties of Citizenship
Promote unity, respect the rights of others, and support national heritage and public institutions with integrity.
A. Youth Constitution Circles
Regular meetings in schools and community centers facilitated by legal educators and youth representatives. Topics: “Understanding the Constitution,” “Justice and Restraint,” “Law as a Legacy.”
B. Pledge of Constitutional Guardianship
Formal Commitment
“In commitment to unity, dignity, and remembrance, I pledge to uphold the Constitution of South Sudan—not as a tool of division, but as a foundation of healing. I commit to protecting the rights of all citizens, acting with restraint, and leading my community with honor.”
C. Mobile Constitutional Outreach
Legal education in rural and flood-affected regions, simplified constitutional materials in local languages, and on-site civic ceremonies and mediation.
