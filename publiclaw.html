Practice: Public Law

Where Sovereignty Meets Service: Balancing Authority with Responsibility

At ICL, we view Public Law as the foundational contract between government and citizens. It guides how institutions must act within defined limits, how officials serve with integrity, and how citizens' rights are safeguarded in accordance with the Constitution. We approach Public Law as the framework that upholds national trust, transforming legality into a lasting legacy and governance into a demonstration of responsible leadership.

Public Law governs the legal relationship between individuals and the state. Its essential functions include:
  • Ensuring constitutional governance and adherence to the rule of law
  • Protecting civil liberties and human rights
  • Holding public institutions and officials accountable
  • Administering justice, public finance, and security lawfully
  • Maintaining appropriate restraint in the exercise of state power
In South Sudan, Public Law is anchored in the Transitional Constitution and supported by statutes including the Civil Service Act, Public Financial Management Act, and Anti-Corruption Commission Act.

  • Article 5 – Primacy of the Constitution and the rule of law
  • Article 11 – Rights to life and human dignity
  • Article 19 – Rights to fair trial and due process
  • Articles 24–27 – Freedoms of expression, assembly, belief, and movement
  • Articles 118–120 – Code of conduct for public officials

  • Public Financial Management and Accountability Act
  • Civil Service Act
  • Anti-Corruption Commission Act
  • Public Grievances Act
  • National Security Service Act
  • Local Government Act
  • Judiciary Act and Judicial Service Council Act

  • Constitutional Advisory and Oversight — Supporting public institutions to operate within lawful and ethical standards
  • Legal Drafting for Public Service — Developing codes of conduct, service charters, and declarations of restraint
  • Administrative Justice and Grievance Resolution — Protecting citizens from unlawful administrative decisions and misconduct
  • Civic Education and Rights Advocacy for Youth — Fostering dignity and civic responsibility across generations
  • Institutional Reform and Governance Enhancement — Strengthening governance frameworks rooted in legal integrity

We believe:
  • The law is a covenant of trust, not merely control
  • Public service is an act of remembrance and responsibility
  • Good governance requires restraint, not domination
  • Every statute should safeguard the dignity of the citizen, not just the authority of the state
CSR Initiative

Know Your Constitution Powered by ICL

To empower South Sudanese youth and communities with comprehensive knowledge of their constitutional rights and responsibilities, emphasizing the vital role of law in fostering national unity, dignity, and intergenerational reconciliation.

The Constitution as a Covenant
A foundational agreement between the people and the state; a framework for restraint, justice, and emotionally intelligent leadership.
Rights and Freedoms
Protecting life, dignity, equality, and fundamental freedoms; access to education, healthcare, and justice for all.
Duties of Citizenship
Promote unity, respect the rights of others, and support national heritage and public institutions with integrity.
A. Youth Constitution Circles
Regular meetings in schools and community centers facilitated by legal educators and youth representatives. Topics: “Understanding the Constitution,” “Justice and Restraint,” “Law as a Legacy.”
B. Pledge of Constitutional Guardianship
Formal Commitment
“In commitment to unity, dignity, and remembrance, I pledge to uphold the Constitution of South Sudan—not as a tool of division, but as a foundation of healing. I commit to protecting the rights of all citizens, acting with restraint, and leading my community with honor.”
C. Mobile Constitutional Outreach
Legal education in rural and flood-affected regions, simplified constitutional materials in local languages, and on-site civic ceremonies and mediation.
