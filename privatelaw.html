Practice: Private Law

Where Agreements Evolve into Covenants and Rights Are Safeguarded with Respect

At ICL, our approach to Private Law extends beyond mere transactions; it emphasizes relationships and trust. This area of law oversees the respectful and dignified interactions between individuals, families, businesses, and communities. We view private legal matters as significant engagements, where preserving dignity, resolving disputes amicably, and honoring commitments are paramount.

Private Law governs the legal relationships between individuals and entities. Its primary objectives include:
  • Protecting personal and property rights
  • Ensuring fairness in contracts, commerce, and inheritance matters
  • Facilitating dispute resolution through restorative and conciliatory processes
  • Recognizing customary norms within private dealings
  • Upholding dignity in family, land, and business transactions
In South Sudan, Private Law is influenced by statutory codes, customary practices, and constitutional principles—including civil law, family law, property law, commercial law, and obligations arising from private agreements.

  • Contract Act (2008) — Governs enforceable agreements
  • Companies Act (2012) — Regulates corporate entities
  • Land Act (2009) — Protects land ownership and transfer processes
  • Inheritance and Succession Laws — Integrate statutory and customary norms
  • Sale of Goods Act (2011) — Promotes fairness in trade transactions
  • Partnership Act (2008) and Limited Partnerships Act (2008) — Define business relationships
  • Consumer Protection Act (2011) — Safeguards individual rights in transactions

  • Practices related to customary marriage, dowry, and inheritance
  • Community land rights and lineage-based property claims
  • Oral agreements and dispute resolution mechanisms involving elders

  • Formal Contract Drafting — Developing agreements that uphold dignity, cultural values, and clarity
  • Dispute Resolution and Mediation — Facilitating reconciliation and settlement through respectful processes
  • Family and Inheritance Advisory — Aligning statutory legal requirements with customary practices
  • Business Formation and Governance — Structuring companies and partnerships with integrity and respect
  • Land and Property Rights Protection — Assisting vulnerable groups in securing their rights

We hold that:
  • A contract is more than a transaction; it is a covenant rooted in trust
  • Disputes are opportunities for healing and understanding
  • Property should be viewed as a stewardship, not merely possession
  • Private law should preserve the essence of relationships, ensuring dignity and respect in every engagement
CSR Initiative

Know Your Constitution Powered by ICL

To empower South Sudanese youth and communities with comprehensive knowledge of their constitutional rights and responsibilities, emphasizing the vital role of law in fostering national unity, dignity, and intergenerational reconciliation.

The Constitution as a Covenant
A foundational agreement between the people and the state; a framework for restraint, justice, and emotionally intelligent leadership.
Rights and Freedoms
Protecting life, dignity, equality, and fundamental freedoms; access to education, healthcare, and justice for all.
Duties of Citizenship
Promote unity, respect the rights of others, and support national heritage and public institutions with integrity.
A. Youth Constitution Circles
Regular meetings in schools and community centers facilitated by legal educators and youth representatives. Topics: “Understanding the Constitution,” “Justice and Restraint,” “Law as a Legacy.”
B. Pledge of Constitutional Guardianship
Formal Commitment
“In commitment to unity, dignity, and remembrance, I pledge to uphold the Constitution of South Sudan—not as a tool of division, but as a foundation of healing. I commit to protecting the rights of all citizens, acting with restraint, and leading my community with honor.”
C. Mobile Constitutional Outreach
Legal education in rural and flood-affected regions, simplified constitutional materials in local languages, and on-site civic ceremonies and mediation.
